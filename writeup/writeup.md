## 8. Open questions / next steps I find compelling

**Subcortical contribution to the BCHE / limbic story.** Both Jun (2022) and Jun (2025) restrict analyses to 139 cortical and subcortical components from the d=300 ICA. The Limbic ICN is the only cross-state spatial feature that hinted at heritability. Yet Jun (2025) found the BCHE polymorphism (gene encoding  expressed densely in hippocampus and amygdala) carrying significant weight in the Transition Probability model. This brings up an unresolved question: a limbic gene shapes connectome transitions, but the limbic ICN is the spatial feature whose heritability is least robust. A natural extension would be to augment the cortical parcellation with a finer-grained subcortical atlas (like Tian et al. 2020) and re-fit the HMM, then ask whether transition probabilities involving subcortical-heavy states specifically carry the BCHE association. This would directly test whether the BCHE finding reflects subcortical dynamics being mis-summarized into cortical states by the d=300 parcellation.

**EEG correlates of HMM-derived connectome states.** Jun (2025) is fMRI-only, but the CONNECT lab's broader work uses simultaneous EEG-fMRI. An open question is whether the four (or six) discrete connectome states recovered by an HMM on BOLD data have stable spectral signatures in concurrently recorded EEG. If so, whether EEG-derived state inference can recover Fractional Occupancy and Transition Probability with sufficient fidelity to be useful as standalone phenotypes. If validated, this would extend the heritability and neurotransmitter-genotype framings to settings where fMRI is impractical: long recordings, non-naturalistic paradigms, populations harder to scan. The HMM-MAR framework was originally developed on MEG/EEG (Vidaurre et al. 2016) before being applied to fMRI, so the methodology is theoretically bidirectional.

**Causal extension via pharmacological intervention.** Jun (2025) establishes that noradrenergic alpha-1 receptor genotypes (ADRA1A, ADRA1D) explain Fractional Occupancy variance, but the design is necessarily correlational. A causal version of the same question would administer a selective alpha-1 antagonist (e.g., prazosin, already used clinically for PTSD per Datta et al. 2019) and measure whether Fractional Occupancy reorganizes in the direction predicted by the genotype model.
